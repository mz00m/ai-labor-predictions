## Competitive Landscape

### Why clients pay $5-25k when Tableau exists
1. **Storytelling vs. dashboards** — Tableau makes grids of charts. You make narratives with scrollytelling, progressive disclosure, guided tours
2. **Brand control** — custom React/D3 matches their visual identity, doesn't scream "BI tool"
3. **Embeddability** — native web components, no clunky Tableau embeds
4. **No licensing fees** — Tableau Server/Cloud is expensive for public-facing use
5. **Creative ceiling** — animated transitions, custom interactions, linked views — impossible in Tableau
6. **The full stack** — most clients' data isn't ready. You wrangle + visualize

## Go-to-Market: The Danny Yang Model

Danny Yang (Meta engineer) freelanced D3.js dataviz evenings/weekends, capping at 10 hrs/week. His playbook:

### Rules of engagement
1. **Cap at 10 hrs/week** — protect the day job and your sanity
2. **Async-first** — tell clients upfront: work done off-hours, 24hr response time
3. **Niche hard** — "interactive data visualization for research & policy" (not "dashboards")
4. **GitHub Pages demos** — clients see live progress without meetings

### Client acquisition (ranked by effectiveness)
1. **Portfolio-as-marketing** — jobsdata.ai IS your portfolio. Publish, share, let inbound come
2. **Referrals** — cross-refer with complementary freelancers (designers, researchers, copywriters)
3. **LinkedIn** — share process posts, case studies. Don't sell; build visibility
4. **Data Viz Society community** — DVS Slack, Observable community, conferences
5. **Upwork** (for early reps) — build a track record, then graduate to direct clients
6. **Direct outreach to think tanks/NGOs** — most are years behind newsrooms in data storytelling

### The Pudding model (longer-term)
Publish editorial data visualizations (like jobsdata.ai) → inbound client inquiries follow. The Pudding funds their entire editorial operation from "a handful of client projects" per year.

## Key Insight

The market is bifurcated. Commodity dashboard work (Power BI, Tableau) is being squeezed by AI tools and offshore freelancers. But **custom interactive data storytelling for research/policy organizations** is a well-funded niche with established buyers who routinely pay $10-50k and value domain expertise + editorial quality.

You're positioned at the intersection of:
- **Domain expertise** (labor economics, policy data)
- **Technical chops** (Next.js, React, Recharts, D3)
- **AI leverage** (5-10x faster delivery than competitors)
- **A live portfolio** (jobsdata.ai proves you can do the work)

That combination is rare. Most dataviz freelancers have 1-2 of these. You have all four.
